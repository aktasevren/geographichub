# 10 Turning-Point Wars — Shortlist

## 1. Second Punic War (-218–-201)
- **slug:** second-punic-war
- **era:** ancient
- **region:** Europe
- **why turning point:** Rome's victory over Carthage ended Carthaginian power in the western Mediterranean and set Rome on the path to empire, reshaping the political order of the ancient world.
- **expected event count:** 22

## 2. Mongol Conquests (1206–1279)
- **slug:** mongol-conquests
- **era:** medieval
- **region:** Asia
- **why turning point:** Genghis Khan and his successors built the largest contiguous land empire in history, toppling the Khwarazmian, Jin, Song, and Abbasid states and redrawing the map of Eurasia from Korea to Hungary.
- **expected event count:** 30

## 3. Thirty Years' War (1618–1648)
- **slug:** thirty-years-war
- **era:** early-modern
- **region:** Europe
- **why turning point:** The deadliest European war before the 20th century ended with the Peace of Westphalia, which established the modern system of sovereign states and broke Habsburg dominance over central Europe.
- **expected event count:** 28

## 4. Napoleonic Wars (1803–1815)
- **slug:** napoleonic-wars
- **era:** 19th-century
- **region:** Europe
- **why turning point:** Napoleon's campaigns dismantled the Holy Roman Empire, spread the Napoleonic Code across the continent, and their defeat produced the Congress of Vienna order that shaped Europe until 1914.
- **expected event count:** 35

## 5. American Civil War (1861–1865)
- **slug:** american-civil-war
- **era:** 19th-century
- **region:** Americas
- **why turning point:** The Union's victory ended chattel slavery in the United States, settled the question of secession, and launched the US on a trajectory toward becoming a continental industrial power.
- **expected event count:** 30

## 6. World War I (1914–1918)
- **slug:** world-war-i
- **era:** 20th-century
- **region:** global
- **why turning point:** The war destroyed four empires (German, Austro-Hungarian, Ottoman, Russian), birthed the Soviet state, redrew the map of Europe and the Middle East, and set the stage for World War II.
- **expected event count:** 40

## 7. Turkish War of Independence (1919–1923)
- **slug:** turkish-war-of-independence
- **era:** 20th-century
- **region:** Middle East
- **why turning point:** The Kemalist victory overturned the Treaty of Sevres, ended the Ottoman Sultanate and Caliphate, and founded the Republic of Turkey, reshaping the post-Ottoman Middle East.
- **expected event count:** 22

## 8. World War II (1939–1945)
- **slug:** world-war-ii
- **era:** 20th-century
- **region:** global
- **why turning point:** The deadliest conflict in history ended European colonial primacy, produced the United Nations order, divided Europe and Korea, and inaugurated the nuclear age and the US-Soviet bipolar system.
- **expected event count:** 50

## 9. Gulf War and Iraq War (1990–2011)
- **slug:** gulf-and-iraq-wars
- **era:** 21st-century
- **region:** Middle East
- **why turning point:** The twin US-led campaigns ended Saddam Hussein's regime, entrenched a unipolar post-Cold War order before straining it, and reshaped the politics and sectarian balance of the modern Middle East.
- **expected event count:** 25

## 10. Russo-Ukrainian War (2014–)
- **slug:** russo-ukrainian-war
- **era:** 21st-century
- **region:** Europe
- **why turning point:** The first large-scale European war since 1945 has redrawn borders in Ukraine, ended the post-Cold War European security order, expanded NATO (Finland, Sweden), and reshaped global energy and sanctions regimes.
- **expected event count:** 30

## Balance notes

**Era coverage:** Each required era is represented: ancient (Second Punic War), medieval (Mongol Conquests), early-modern (Thirty Years' War), 19th-century (Napoleonic Wars, American Civil War), 20th-century (WWI, Turkish War of Independence, WWII), 21st-century (Gulf/Iraq Wars, Russo-Ukrainian War).

**Region coverage:** Europe (Second Punic War, Thirty Years' War, Napoleonic Wars, Russo-Ukrainian War), Middle East (Turkish War of Independence, Gulf/Iraq Wars), Asia (Mongol Conquests), Americas (American Civil War), global (WWI, WWII). All required regions are covered.

**Existing six:** All six preserved — Turkish War of Independence, World War I, World War II, American Civil War, Napoleonic Wars, and Russo-Ukrainian War all qualify as turning-point wars with rich geocodable event sets.

**New additions:** Second Punic War, Mongol Conquests, Thirty Years' War, and the combined Gulf/Iraq Wars fill the ancient, medieval, early-modern, and 21st-century Middle East gaps respectively.

**Event density:** Every entry is expected to yield at least 20 geocodable events (battles, sieges, treaties, landings). WWII and WWI are the richest (~40-50); Second Punic War and Turkish War of Independence are the leanest but still comfortably above the threshold.

**Notes and caveats:**
- The Second Punic War uses negative years (BCE) for start/end; the map timeline will need to handle BCE dates for this entry.
- The Mongol Conquests span multiple campaigns (Khwarazmia, Jin/Song China, Middle East, Europe) across 73 years — it is treated as a single "war" for documentary purposes, consistent with how most survey works present it.
- Entry 9 combines the Gulf War (1990-91), Iraq War (2003-11), and arguably the interwar no-fly-zone period into one documentary arc; this may be split into two entries if per-war scoping is preferred during implementation.
- The Russo-Ukrainian War is ongoing (end year open); events will need a rolling update policy.
